If you've ever left a doctor's appointment with a prescription but no sense of *why* you felt unwell in the first place, you've probably wondered if there's another way to practice medicine.

There is. It's called integrative general practice, and it's quickly becoming one of the fastest-growing areas of primary care in Australia.

## The Short Answer

An integrative GP is a fully qualified medical doctor — trained in exactly the same way as any other GP — who also looks at the bigger picture of your health. That means nutrition, sleep, stress, movement, relationships, and the environment you live in. It means asking *why* your body is doing what it's doing, not just suppressing the symptom.

It's not alternative medicine. It's not anti-medicine. It's medicine *plus* — with the rigour of evidence-based training and the humility to recognise that a human body is more than a collection of symptoms.

## What Makes It Different

A standard 10-minute GP consultation is designed for efficiency. You come in with a symptom, you leave with a plan — usually a prescription, a test, or a referral. That model works beautifully for acute problems like an infection or a broken ankle.

But it struggles with the conditions that make up most of modern ill-health: chronic fatigue, hormonal imbalance, persistent anxiety, gut issues, autoimmune flares, burnout. These don't have neat fixes. They have roots.

Integrative GPs typically offer:

- **Longer appointments** — often 30 to 60 minutes for new patients, because listening takes time.
- **A root-cause lens** — asking what's driving the symptom rather than just managing it.
- **Lifestyle medicine** — evidence-based attention to sleep, nutrition, movement, stress, and circadian health.
- **Collaboration** — often working alongside naturopaths, nutritionists, psychologists and other allied health professionals.
- **Patient education** — because knowledge is freeing, and you are the person who lives in your body every day.

## What It Isn't

It's worth being clear about what integrative medicine is not. It's not:

- A rejection of conventional medicine. Integrative GPs still prescribe, refer, order pathology, and handle the full scope of general practice.
- A replacement for specialists. If you need a cardiologist, you'll get a cardiologist.
- Unregulated. An integrative GP in Australia is still a FRACGP-qualified doctor, registered with AHPRA, practising within the same legal and ethical framework as every other GP.

The "integrative" part is about widening the lens, not lowering the bar.

## Who It's For

Integrative general practice is particularly well-suited to people who:

- Have symptoms that conventional medicine has struggled to explain.
- Want to take a proactive, preventive approach to their health.
- Are navigating hormonal health, perimenopause, or fertility.
- Are managing chronic conditions and want more than just symptom control.
- Simply want a doctor who has time to listen.

It's also a fantastic fit for families — children, in particular, benefit enormously from a practitioner who takes the whole environment into account.

## The Bottom Line

If you want medicine that sees you as a whole person and treats the root of your health concerns rather than just the surface, an integrative GP might be exactly what you're looking for. It's not magic. It's just medicine, practised with more time, more listening, and a wider lens.
